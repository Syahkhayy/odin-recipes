Mee Kari

Description

A very delicious recipe of Mee Kari (Curry Mee) from Malaysia and very popular amongst hawker's dish. Mee Kari is a dish that full of flavours with slightly creamy soup infused with coconut milk.

Ingredients

  • 500 g (17 oz.) prawns, steamed and shelled; use the heads to sweeten the stock by liquidising them with 500ml water
  • 3 to 4 pieces soaked cuttlefish heads
  • 200 g (7 oz.) fried soya bean cubes/ tow pok, halved or quartered
  • 300 g (10 oz.) shredded, cooked chicken meat
  • 500 g (17 oz.) blanched bean sprouts
  • 600 g (21 oz.) blanched yellow noodles
  • 300 g (10 oz.) blanched vermicelli/rice sticks
  • 1 kg (35 oz.) grated coconut, mixed with 4 litres water and squeezed for the coconut milk to be used as main stock
  • 600 g (21 oz.) shelled cockles/bloody clams, optional

Spices (Finely Ground)

  • 100 g (4 oz.)shallots
  • 25 g (1 oz.) garlic
  • 3 tablespoons coriander seeds
  • 4 tablespoons chili paste
  • 2 tablespoons lemongrass
  • 10 peppercorns
  • 1/2 tablespoon belacan, Malaysian shrimp paste granules

Steps

  1. Heat 1/2 cup oil to saute the spices till fragrant.
  2. Add in salt, rock sugar and 500ml general santan and bring to a low boil till sugar dissolves.
  3. Add in the rest of the coconut milk, tow pok.
  4. When soup comes to a boil, add prawn stock and seasoning, then bring to just boiling point.
  5. Remove from fire and use stock as a soup for the yellow noodles and vermicelli.
